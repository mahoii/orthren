**CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT**

Rachel Kim is a patient presenting with right shoulder pain and weakness following a work-related lifting injury, with symptoms persisting over eight months. She reports pain rated 7/10 at rest and 9/10 with overhead activity, reflecting a significant and sustained pain burden that has failed to resolve with conservative management. Her functional deficits are severe, precluding return to occupational duties and substantially limiting her ability to perform activities of daily living.

**DIAGNOSIS**

M75.121 — Complete rotator cuff tear, right shoulder
M75.111 — Incomplete rotator cuff tear, right shoulder

**FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS**

1. Unable to lift objects above shoulder height
2. Cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks
3. Disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain
4. Unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation

**CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY**

1. **Physical Therapy** (September–October 2024, 8 weeks): Ms. Kim completed an eight-week course of physical therapy. The patient experienced minimal improvement, with pain persisting with overhead activity. Functional goals were not achieved.

2. **Corticosteroid Injection — Kenalog 40mg** (11/05/2024): A single corticosteroid injection was administered. The patient experienced two weeks of partial relief, after which symptoms returned to baseline.

3. **NSAIDs — Meloxicam 15mg daily** (July–October 2024, 3 months): The patient was maintained on meloxicam 15mg daily for three months. This regimen provided inadequate pain control, and GI intolerance developed, necessitating discontinuation.

**REQUESTED PROCEDURE**

Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair with mini-open rotator cuff repair, right shoulder (CPT 29827).

The patient's BMI of 27.4 and ASA II classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan. The patient carries an ASA II classification, reflecting the anesthetic risk profile accounted for in the perioperative surgical plan.

Imaging obtained on 01/22/2025 provides objective structural confirmation of the surgical indication. Plain radiographs demonstrate Grade II AC joint arthrosis with no calcific deposits and no significant glenohumeral arthrosis. MRI of the right shoulder reveals a full-thickness supraspinatus tear measuring 2.1 cm with retraction to the musculotendinous junction, accompanied by moderate subacromial bursitis. The subscapularis is intact. These findings are consistent with the severity of the patient's clinical presentation and confirm a structural lesion that is not amenable to further conservative management.

Physical examination corroborates the imaging findings. Active forward flexion is limited to 95 degrees by pain, and active abduction is restricted to 80 degrees. Internal rotation measures 40 degrees and external rotation 55 degrees. Strength testing demonstrates 3/5 supraspinatus strength on the empty can test, indicating significant rotator cuff dysfunction. The patient's American Shoulder and Elbow Surgeons (ASES) score of 35.2 out of 100 reflects severe functional impairment and is well below the threshold associated with acceptable shoulder function.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY**

Rachel Kim presents with a confirmed full-thickness supraspinatus tear measuring 2.1 cm with musculotendinous junction retraction, substantiated by MRI and corroborated by objective physical examination findings including markedly restricted range of motion, 3/5 supraspinatus strength, and an ASES score of 35.2/100. Conservative management to date has included eight weeks of physical therapy with minimal improvement, a corticosteroid injection yielding only two weeks of partial relief, and three months of NSAID therapy that was discontinued due to inadequate efficacy and GI intolerance. Despite this course of treatment, Ms. Kim remains unable to perform overhead reaching, lift objects above shoulder height, sleep without pain-related disruption, or return to her occupational duties. The natural history of full-thickness rotator cuff tears of this size and degree of retraction is one of progressive enlargement and irreversible muscle atrophy without surgical intervention. Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair with mini-open technique represents the medically necessary and appropriate next step to restore shoulder function, prevent further structural deterioration, and enable this patient to return to her activities of daily living and occupational responsibilities.
