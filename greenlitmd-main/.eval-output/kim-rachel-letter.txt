Dear Prior Authorization Reviewer,

CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT

This patient presents with an eight-month history of right shoulder pain and weakness following a work-related lifting injury, reporting pain rated 7/10 at rest and 9/10 with overhead activity. The patient is unable to lift objects above shoulder height, cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks, experiences disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain, and is unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation. Range of motion assessment demonstrates active forward flexion limited to 95 degrees with pain, active abduction of 80 degrees, internal rotation of 40 degrees, and external rotation of 55 degrees. Strength testing reveals 3/5 supraspinatus strength on empty can test, and the patient's American Shoulder and Elbow Surgeons (ASES) score is 35.2 out of 100, reflecting severe functional impairment.

DIAGNOSIS

The patient carries diagnoses of complete rotator cuff tear of the right shoulder (M75.121) and incomplete rotator cuff tear of the right shoulder (M75.111), confirmed by advanced imaging as detailed below.

FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS

1. Unable to lift objects above shoulder height
2. Cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks
3. Disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain
4. Unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation

CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY

1. Physical Therapy — The patient completed an eight-week course of physical therapy. Improvement was minimal, and pain persisted with overhead activity.

2. Corticosteroid Injection (Kenalog 40mg) — The patient received a corticosteroid injection, which provided partial relief for approximately two weeks, after which symptoms returned to baseline.

3. NSAIDs — Meloxicam 15mg Daily — The patient was maintained on meloxicam 15mg daily for three months. This regimen provided inadequate pain control, and the patient developed gastrointestinal intolerance, necessitating discontinuation.

REQUESTED PROCEDURE

Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair, right shoulder (CPT 29827), utilizing an arthroscopic with mini-open rotator cuff repair approach. The patient's BMI of 27.4 and ASA II classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan.

MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY

Conservative management to date has included an eight-week course of physical therapy, a corticosteroid injection, and three months of NSAID therapy. Each modality has been pursued without achieving durable or meaningful relief. The structural severity of a full-thickness supraspinatus tear with retraction to the musculotendinous junction, combined with an ASES score of 35.2/100, objective strength deficits of 3/5 on supraspinatus testing, and significantly restricted range of motion, establishes that continued non-operative management is unlikely to restore function or prevent further tendon retraction and muscle atrophy. Surgical intervention is medically necessary to restore this patient's ability to maintain activities of daily living, return to occupational function, and prevent progressive structural deterioration of the rotator cuff.
