**CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT**

Rachel Kim is a 50-year-old female presenting with right shoulder pain and weakness following a work-related lifting injury sustained in June 2024, with symptoms persisting over eight months. She reports pain rated 7/10 at rest and 9/10 with overhead activity, with functional deficits that have precluded return to occupational duties and significantly impaired activities of daily living.

**DIAGNOSIS**

M75.121 — Complete rotator cuff tear, right shoulder
M75.111 — Incomplete rotator cuff tear, right shoulder

**FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS**

1. Unable to lift objects above shoulder height
2. Cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks
3. Disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain
4. Unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation

**CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY**

1. **NSAIDs — Meloxicam 15mg daily** (July–October 2024; 3 months): Meloxicam 15mg daily was initiated for pain management. The patient experienced inadequate pain control, and GI intolerance developed, necessitating discontinuation.

2. **Physical Therapy** (September–October 2024; 8 weeks): The patient completed an eight-week course of formal physical therapy. Minimal improvement was achieved, with pain persisting with overhead activity, and functional goals were not met.

3. **Corticosteroid Injection — Kenalog 40mg** (11/05/2024): A subacromial corticosteroid injection with Kenalog 40mg was administered on November 5, 2024. The patient experienced two weeks of partial relief, after which symptoms returned to baseline.

**REQUESTED PROCEDURE**

Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair, right shoulder (CPT 29827). Surgical Approach: Arthroscopic with mini-open rotator cuff repair.

The patient's BMI of 27.4 and ASA II classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan. The patient carries an ASA II classification, reflecting the anesthetic risk profile accounted for in the perioperative surgical plan.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY**

Rachel Kim presents with a confirmed full-thickness supraspinatus tear measuring 2.1 cm with retraction to the musculotendinous junction, as demonstrated on MRI obtained on January 22, 2025. Radiographs of the right shoulder obtained on January 22, 2025 reveal Grade II AC joint arthrosis without calcific deposits and no significant glenohumeral arthrosis. Moderate subacromial bursitis is also present, with an intact subscapularis tendon.

Physical examination confirms significant objective impairment. Range of motion assessment demonstrates active forward flexion limited to 95 degrees by pain, active abduction of 80 degrees, internal rotation of 40 degrees, and external rotation of 55 degrees. Strength testing reveals 3/5 supraspinatus strength on empty can testing. The patient's American Shoulder and Elbow Surgeons (ASES) score of 35.2 out of 100 reflects severe functional compromise consistent with the clinical presentation.

Conservative management to date has included an eight-week course of physical therapy, a subacromial corticosteroid injection with Kenalog 40mg, and three months of NSAID therapy with Meloxicam 15mg daily. Each modality has been pursued without achieving durable symptomatic relief or functional restoration. The corticosteroid injection provided only two weeks of partial relief before symptoms returned to baseline. Physical therapy produced minimal improvement with persistent pain on overhead activity. Meloxicam was discontinued due to inadequate pain control and the development of GI intolerance.

Given the structural severity of the tear, the objective functional deficits documented on examination, the validated functional impairment reflected in the ASES score, and the failure of multimodal conservative management, arthroscopic rotator cuff repair of the right shoulder is medically necessary and appropriate. Without surgical intervention, Ms. Kim faces continued inability to perform overhead activities of daily living, ongoing sleep disruption, and inability to return to her occupational duties. Approval of this authorization request is respectfully requested.
