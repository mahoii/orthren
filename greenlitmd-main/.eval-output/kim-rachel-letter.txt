Dear Prior Authorization Reviewer,

CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT

This patient presents with an eight-month history of right shoulder pain and weakness following a work-related lifting injury, reporting pain rated 7/10 at rest and 9/10 with overhead activity. Range of motion assessment demonstrates active forward flexion limited to 95 degrees, active abduction to 80 degrees, internal rotation to 40 degrees, and external rotation to 55 degrees, with strength testing revealing 3/5 supraspinatus strength on empty can test and an ASES Score of 35.2 out of 100. The patient is unable to lift objects above shoulder height, cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks, experiences disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain, and is unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation.

DIAGNOSIS

The patient carries confirmed diagnoses of complete rotator cuff tear of the right shoulder (M75.121) and incomplete rotator cuff tear of the right shoulder (M75.111), supported by clinical examination and advanced imaging as detailed below.

FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS

1. Unable to lift objects above shoulder height
2. Cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks
3. Disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain
4. Unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation

CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY

1. Physical Therapy (September through the conclusion of that course, 8 weeks): The patient underwent an eight-week course of physical therapy, resulting in minimal improvement with pain persisting throughout overhead activity.

2. Corticosteroid Injection — Kenalog 40mg: A corticosteroid injection was administered, providing two weeks of partial relief before symptoms returned to baseline.

3. NSAIDs — Meloxicam 15mg daily (July through the conclusion of that course, 3 months): The patient was maintained on meloxicam 15mg daily for three months; this regimen provided inadequate pain control, and GI intolerance subsequently developed, necessitating discontinuation.

REQUESTED PROCEDURE

Arthroscopic with mini-open rotator cuff repair, right shoulder (CPT 29827).

Imaging obtained confirms the structural basis for surgical intervention. X-ray demonstrates Grade II AC joint arthrosis with no calcific deposits and no significant glenohumeral arthrosis. MRI reveals a full-thickness supraspinatus tear measuring 2.1 cm with retraction to the musculotendinous junction, accompanied by moderate subacromial bursitis, with the subscapularis confirmed intact. These findings are consistent with the patient's clinical presentation and support the need for operative repair to restore rotator cuff integrity and prevent further tendon retraction and muscle atrophy.

The patient's BMI of 27.4 and ASA II classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan.

MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY

Conservative management to date has included an eight-week course of physical therapy, a corticosteroid injection, and three months of NSAID therapy, none of which has provided durable relief or restored functional capacity. The structural severity of a 2.1 cm full-thickness supraspinatus tear with retraction, combined with an ASES Score of 35.2/100 and the documented functional deficits outlined above, establishes that continued non-operative management is unlikely to achieve meaningful recovery. Surgical intervention is medically necessary to restore the patient's ability to maintain activities of daily living, return to occupational function, and prevent progressive deterioration of the rotator cuff musculature. Authorization for CPT 29827 is respectfully requested.
