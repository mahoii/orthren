**CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT**

Rachel Kim presents with right shoulder pain and weakness following a work-related lifting injury, with symptoms persisting over an eight-month course. She reports pain rated 7/10 at rest and 9/10 with overhead activity, reflecting a significant and persistent pain burden that has failed to resolve with conservative management. Her functional deficits have substantially impaired her ability to perform activities of daily living and have precluded her return to occupational duties requiring arm elevation.

**DIAGNOSIS**

M75.121 — Complete rotator cuff tear, right shoulder
M75.111 — Incomplete rotator cuff tear, right shoulder

**FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS**

1. Unable to lift objects above shoulder height
2. Cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks
3. Disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain
4. Unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation

**CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY**

1. **Physical Therapy** (September–October 2024, 8 weeks): Ms. Kim completed a structured course of physical therapy over eight weeks. The patient experienced minimal improvement, with pain persisting with overhead activity. Functional improvement was insufficient to restore meaningful mobility, and therapy was ultimately discontinued without achieving treatment goals.

2. **Corticosteroid Injection — Kenalog 40mg** (administered 11/05/2024): A subacromial corticosteroid injection was administered. The patient experienced two weeks of partial relief, after which symptoms returned to baseline. The injection trial is considered a failed conservative intervention.

3. **NSAIDs — Meloxicam 15mg daily** (July–October 2024, 3 months): The patient was maintained on Meloxicam 15mg daily for three months. This course provided inadequate pain control, and GI intolerance developed, necessitating discontinuation.

**REQUESTED PROCEDURE**

Arthroscopic with mini-open rotator cuff repair, right shoulder (CPT 29827).

The patient's BMI of 27.4 and ASA II classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan. The patient carries an ASA II classification, reflecting the anesthetic risk profile accounted for in the perioperative surgical plan.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY**

Objective clinical assessment confirms the severity of Ms. Kim's right shoulder pathology. Range of motion evaluation demonstrates active forward flexion limited to 95 degrees secondary to pain, active abduction of 80 degrees, internal rotation of 40 degrees, and external rotation of 55 degrees. Strength testing reveals 3/5 supraspinatus strength on the empty can test, consistent with significant rotator cuff dysfunction. The American Shoulder and Elbow Surgeons (ASES) Score of 35.2 out of 100 further quantifies the degree of functional impairment.

Imaging obtained confirms the structural basis for these deficits. Radiographs demonstrate Grade II acromioclavicular joint arthrosis with no calcific deposits and no significant glenohumeral arthrosis. MRI demonstrates a full-thickness supraspinatus tear measuring 2.1 cm with retraction to the musculotendinous junction, accompanied by moderate subacromial bursitis, with an intact subscapularis tendon. The size and retraction of the tear are consistent with a lesion that carries a risk of further propagation without surgical intervention, and the structural findings correlate directly with the patient's clinical presentation.

Conservative management to date has included eight weeks of formal physical therapy, a corticosteroid injection with only transient partial relief, and a three-month course of NSAIDs that was discontinued due to inadequate efficacy and GI intolerance. Despite this multimodal conservative course, Ms. Kim continues to experience severe pain, marked range of motion deficits, and functional limitations that prevent her from performing overhead activities of daily living and from fulfilling her occupational responsibilities. Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair represents the appropriate and necessary next step to restore shoulder function, relieve pain, and enable Ms. Kim's return to baseline activity and employment.
