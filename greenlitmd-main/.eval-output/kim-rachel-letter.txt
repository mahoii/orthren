Dear Prior Authorization Reviewer,

CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT

This patient presents with an eight-month history of right shoulder pain and weakness following a work-related lifting injury, reporting pain rated 7/10 at rest and 9/10 with overhead activity. The patient is unable to lift objects above shoulder height, cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks, experiences disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain, and is unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation.

Range of motion assessment demonstrates active forward flexion limited to 95 degrees with pain, active abduction of 80 degrees, internal rotation of 40 degrees, and external rotation of 55 degrees. Strength testing reveals 3/5 supraspinatus strength on empty can testing. The American Shoulder and Elbow Surgeons (ASES) score is 35.2 out of 100, reflecting severe functional compromise.

DIAGNOSIS

The patient carries diagnoses of complete rotator cuff tear of the right shoulder (M75.121) and incomplete rotator cuff tear of the right shoulder (M75.111), confirmed by clinical examination and advanced imaging as detailed below.

FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS

1. Unable to lift objects above shoulder height
2. Cannot perform overhead reaching for daily tasks
3. Disturbed sleep due to shoulder pain
4. Unable to return to work duties requiring arm elevation

CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY

1. Physical Therapy (September through the conclusion of that course, spanning 8 weeks): The patient underwent a structured course of physical therapy. Minimal improvement was achieved, with pain persisting throughout overhead activity. Therapy was ultimately discontinued without achieving treatment goals.

2. Corticosteroid Injection — Kenalog 40mg: A subacromial corticosteroid injection was administered. The patient experienced two weeks of partial relief, after which symptoms returned to baseline.

3. NSAIDs — Meloxicam 15mg daily (July through the end of the treatment course, spanning 3 months): The patient was maintained on meloxicam 15mg daily. This regimen provided inadequate pain control, and GI intolerance subsequently developed, necessitating discontinuation.

Conservative management to date has included physical therapy, corticosteroid injection, and NSAID therapy.

REQUESTED PROCEDURE

Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair with mini-open technique, right shoulder (CPT 29827).

The patient's BMI of 27.4 and ASA II classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan.

Imaging obtained confirms the structural basis for surgical intervention. X-ray obtained on 01/22/2025 demonstrates Grade II acromioclavicular joint arthrosis with no calcific deposits and no significant glenohumeral arthrosis. MRI obtained on 01/22/2025 reveals a full-thickness supraspinatus tear measuring 2.1 centimeters with retraction to the musculotendinous junction, accompanied by moderate subacromial bursitis. The subscapularis is intact. These findings are consistent with the patient's clinical presentation and are not amenable to further conservative management.

MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY

This patient has sustained a structurally confirmed, full-thickness supraspinatus tear of the right shoulder that has failed to respond to an eight-month course of multimodal conservative management, including physical therapy, corticosteroid injection, and NSAID therapy. Objective measurements document severely restricted range of motion, significant supraspinatus weakness at 3/5 on strength testing, and an ASES score of 35.2/100, collectively reflecting profound functional impairment. The patient's inability to maintain activities of daily living, combined with the documented failure of conservative care and the structural severity confirmed on MRI, establishes clear medical necessity for arthroscopic rotator cuff repair of the right shoulder. Timely surgical intervention is requested to prevent further tendon retraction, muscle atrophy, and irreversible functional decline.
