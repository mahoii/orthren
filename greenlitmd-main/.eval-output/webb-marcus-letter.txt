CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT

This patient presents with bilateral knee pain, right greater than left, persisting for more than three years, with progressive functional decline that has rendered the patient unable to walk more than one block, unable to use stairs without a handrail, and unable to demonstrate drills in the patient's role as a volunteer coach. Webb, Marcus D has a documented BMI of 34.2, Class I obesity, which represents a significant contributor to articular cartilage loading and disease progression. Pain is rated 8/10 with activity and 4/10 at rest.

DIAGNOSIS

Primary Diagnosis: M17.11 — Primary osteoarthritis, right knee
Secondary Diagnosis: M17.12 — Primary osteoarthritis, left knee

FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS

1. Cannot walk more than one block.
2. Cannot use stairs without a handrail.
3. Pain limits ability to demonstrate drills as a volunteer coach.

CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY

1. Physical Therapy — The patient completed a six-week course of physical therapy. Functional improvement was insufficient to restore meaningful mobility, and therapy was ultimately discontinued without achieving treatment goals, with no benefit reported for the right knee.

2. Corticosteroid Injection (Cortisone) — Cortisone injections were administered on approximately 2023. Each injection provided only two to three weeks of symptomatic relief, after which pain returned to baseline, rendering this intervention inadequate for sustained management.

3. Hyaluronic Acid Injection (Synvisc-One) — Administered in June 2024, this intervention yielded minimal benefit.

4. Tylenol and Ibuprofen (OTC Analgesics/NSAIDs) — The patient has been using over-the-counter analgesics and NSAIDs on an ongoing basis; these medications have not been effective for weeks and no longer provide meaningful pain relief.

REQUESTED PROCEDURE

Right Total Knee Arthroplasty (CPT 27447), anterior approach, standard primary TKA.

Range of motion assessment of the right knee demonstrates flexion limited to 95 degrees with a flexion contracture of -5 degrees at extension. The patient's KOOS Score for the right knee is 28/100, reflecting severe functional impairment. Varus deformity of 8 degrees is present at the right knee. Radiographs obtained on 02/15/2025 confirm Kellgren-Lawrence Grade IV tricompartmental osteoarthritis of the right knee with bone-on-bone medial compartment involvement and the documented 8-degree varus deformity. The left knee demonstrates Kellgren-Lawrence Grade III osteoarthritis, less severe than the right.

The patient carries an ASA III classification, reflecting the anesthetic risk profile accounted for in the perioperative surgical plan.

MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY

The clinical, functional, and radiographic evidence presented in this letter collectively establishes that right total knee arthroplasty is medically necessary for this patient. More than three years of progressive bilateral knee osteoarthritis, culminating in Kellgren-Lawrence Grade IV disease with bone-on-bone medial compartment involvement and an 8-degree varus deformity of the right knee, has failed to respond to an adequate trial of conservative management including physical therapy, corticosteroid injections, hyaluronic acid injection, and ongoing analgesic use. The patient's KOOS Score of 28/100 and objective range of motion deficits confirm severe functional impairment. The patient is no longer able to maintain activities of daily living at an acceptable level, and surgical intervention represents the only remaining option to restore function and relieve pain.
