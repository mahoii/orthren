**CLINICAL HISTORY AND PRESENTING COMPLAINT**

Marcus Webb is a 67-year-old male presenting with bilateral knee pain, right worse than left, with symptoms ongoing for more than three years, resulting in severely restricted mobility and an inability to perform basic activities of daily living. Mr. Webb has a documented BMI of 34.2, Class I obesity, which represents a significant contributor to articular cartilage loading and disease progression. The severity of his right knee degeneration has rendered him unable to walk more than one block, navigate stairs without a handrail, or fulfill his role as a volunteer coach, as he can no longer physically demonstrate drills to his athletes.

Range of motion assessment of the right knee demonstrates flexion limited to 95 degrees with a flexion contracture of -5 degrees at extension, and a varus deformity measuring 8 degrees. The patient's KOOS Score for the right knee is 28/100, reflecting profound functional impairment. Mr. Webb reports pain rated 8/10 with activity and 4/10 at rest.

**DIAGNOSIS**

Mr. Webb carries a diagnosis of primary osteoarthritis of the right knee (M17.11) and primary osteoarthritis of the left knee (M17.12). Radiographs obtained on February 15, 2025 confirm severe tricompartmental osteoarthritis of the right knee with bone-on-bone medial compartment involvement, Kellgren-Lawrence Grade IV changes, and an 8-degree varus deformity. The left knee demonstrates moderate osteoarthritis with Kellgren-Lawrence Grade III changes, less severe than the right.

**FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS**

1. Cannot walk more than one block due to pain and mechanical limitation of the right knee.
2. Cannot use stairs without a handrail secondary to right knee instability and pain.
3. Pain limits ability to demonstrate drills as a volunteer coach, precluding meaningful participation in a longstanding community role.

**CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT HISTORY**

Mr. Webb has undergone an extended course of conservative management prior to this surgical referral, as detailed below.

1. **Physical Therapy** — Mr. Webb completed a six-week course of formal physical therapy. Functional improvement was insufficient to restore meaningful mobility to the right knee, and therapy was ultimately discontinued without achieving treatment goals.

2. **Corticosteroid Injection (Cortisone)** — The patient received corticosteroid injections approximately in 2023. Each injection provided only transient relief lasting two to three weeks, after which symptoms returned to baseline, demonstrating inadequate and unsustained clinical response.

3. **Hyaluronic Acid Injection — Synvisc-One** — A Synvisc-One hyaluronic acid injection was administered in June 2024. The patient experienced minimal benefit from this intervention.

4. **Acetaminophen (Tylenol) and Ibuprofen/NSAID (OTC)** — The patient has been on an ongoing regimen of over-the-counter acetaminophen and ibuprofen. These medications are no longer providing effective pain relief.

**REQUESTED PROCEDURE**

Right total knee arthroplasty (CPT 27447) is requested for Marcus Webb. The surgical approach will be anterior, with performance of a standard primary total knee arthroplasty. The patient carries an ASA III classification, reflecting the anesthetic risk profile accounted for in the perioperative surgical plan. The patient's BMI of 34.2 and ASA III classification have been factored into the perioperative surgical plan.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY SUMMARY**

Mr. Webb presents with end-stage primary osteoarthritis of the right knee confirmed by radiographic evidence of Kellgren-Lawrence Grade IV changes, bone-on-bone medial compartment involvement, an 8-degree varus deformity, and a KOOS Score of 28/100 — indicative of severe functional impairment. Conservative management to date has included formal physical therapy, corticosteroid injections, hyaluronic acid injection with Synvisc-One, and ongoing oral analgesic therapy, none of which has provided durable or adequate relief. The patient is unable to walk more than one block, cannot navigate stairs without assistive support, and has been forced to abandon meaningful physical activity in his volunteer coaching role. Right total knee arthroplasty represents the appropriate and necessary next intervention to restore functional mobility, alleviate intractable pain, and allow Mr. Webb to resume the activities of daily living that his current condition has rendered impossible. Authorization for CPT 27447 is respectfully requested.
